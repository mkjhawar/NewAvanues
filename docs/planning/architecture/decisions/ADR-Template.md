## ADR Guidelines

### When to Create an ADR
- **Architectural Impact:** Decision affects system structure or key components
- **Significant Cost:** Decision involves substantial time, money, or effort  
- **Hard to Reverse:** Decision would be difficult to change later
- **Alternative Options:** Multiple viable approaches were considered
- **Team Impact:** Decision affects multiple team members or teams
- **Long-term Consequences:** Decision has lasting impact on project

### ADR Lifecycle
1. **Proposed:** Decision identified and documented
2. **Accepted:** Team agrees on decision approach
3. **Implemented:** Decision has been put into practice
4. **Deprecated:** Decision is no longer recommended
5. **Superseded:** Replaced by newer ADR

### Writing Tips
- **Be Specific:** Avoid vague language, provide concrete details
- **Show Your Work:** Document why alternatives were rejected
- **Consider Future You:** Write for someone discovering this decision later
- **Update Status:** Keep implementation status current
- **Link Extensively:** Connect to related documents and decisions
